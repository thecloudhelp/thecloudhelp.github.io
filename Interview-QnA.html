What is Salesforce and how does it work?
Salesforce is a cloud-based customer relationship management (CRM) software that helps businesses manage their sales, customer support, marketing, and other related activities. Salesforce works by providing a single, unified platform for all customer-related activities.

What are the different editions of Salesforce?
Salesforce offers several editions, including the Essentials, Professional, Enterprise, and Unlimited editions. Each edition has different features and pricing options.

What is a record in Salesforce?
A record in Salesforce is a unit of data that represents a single instance of an object, such as an account, contact, or opportunity.

What is an object in Salesforce?
An object in Salesforce is a database table that stores data related to a specific type of record, such as an account, contact, or opportunity.

What is a field in Salesforce?
A field in Salesforce is a specific piece of data that is stored within a record. Fields can be text, numbers, dates, or other data types.

What is a relationship in Salesforce?
A relationship in Salesforce defines how objects are related to each other. For example, an account can have many contacts, so there is a relationship between the account and contact objects.

What is a lookup relationship in Salesforce?
A lookup relationship in Salesforce allows you to associate one record with another record. For example, you can associate a contact with an account.

What is a master-detail relationship in Salesforce?
A master-detail relationship in Salesforce is a more tightly coupled relationship than a lookup relationship. It is used when one object is considered the master and another is considered the detail. For example, an opportunity is a detail object that is associated with an account, which is the master object.
