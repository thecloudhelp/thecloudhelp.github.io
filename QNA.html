What is Salesforce and how do you define it?
  • Salesforce is a cloud-based Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform that allows businesses to manage their customer interactions, sales, marketing, and customer service all in one place. It provides a suite of tools and services that help organizations manage and analyze customer interactions and data, automate processes, and collaborate across departments.
  • In simpler terms, Salesforce is a software solution that helps businesses better understand and manage their customers, which ultimately leads to more effective sales and marketing strategies, improved customer service, and increased customer satisfaction.
  • Salesforce also offers a variety of cloud-based applications and services, including Sales Cloud for managing sales processes, Service Cloud for managing customer service interactions, Marketing Cloud for managing marketing campaigns, and Commerce Cloud for managing online sales.
What is CRM?
  • CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It refers to the strategies, technologies, and practices that organizations use to manage and analyze customer interactions and data throughout the customer lifecycle, with the goal of improving customer relationships, increasing customer retention and loyalty, and ultimately driving sales growth.
  • A CRM system typically includes a database of customer information, as well as tools for managing and tracking customer interactions across various channels such as phone, email, social media, and in-person meetings. CRM systems can also include automation tools for tasks such as lead management, sales forecasting, and marketing campaign management.
  • By implementing a CRM system, organizations can gain valuable insights into customer behavior and preferences, identify opportunities for cross-selling and upselling, and better tailor their products and services to meet the needs of their customers.
What is a profile in Salesforce?
  • In Salesforce, a profile is a collection of settings and permissions that define what a user can see and do within the organization.
  • Profiles are used to determine which records and fields users can access, which tasks they can perform, and which features they can use. They also control the level of data access that users have, which includes defining which records users can view, edit, delete, or share.
  • Profiles can be customized to meet the specific needs of different users or groups of users within an organization. For example, a sales representative might have a profile that allows them to access and edit opportunities, while a customer service representative might have a profile that allows them to view and update customer cases.
  • In addition to profiles, Salesforce also uses permission sets to provide additional levels of access and permissions to users. Permission sets are similar to profiles, but they can be added on top of a user's existing profile to provide additional access to certain features or data.
What is an object in Salesforce?
  • In Salesforce, an object is a table or a database of related fields that store data specific to an organization. Objects represent different types of data, such as Accounts, Contacts, Opportunities, Cases, and custom objects, and are used to organize and manage data in a logical way.
  • Each object contains a set of fields that define the data that can be stored in the object. Fields can be of different types, such as text, number, date, picklist, or lookup fields, and can have specific properties and behaviors that define how data is entered, stored, and used.
  • Objects can be related to other objects through relationships such as lookup or master-detail relationships, which allow data to be shared and linked between objects. Custom objects can also be created to meet specific business needs and can have their own fields, relationships, and behaviors.
  • Overall, objects are a fundamental building block in Salesforce, and they form the foundation of many key features such as reporting, automation, and security. Understanding objects and their relationships is essential to designing effective data models in Salesforce.
What is a record in Salesforce?
  • A record in Salesforce refers to a single instance of an object in the database. An object is a table-like structure that stores specific types of data within the Salesforce system. Examples of objects in Salesforce include accounts, contacts, opportunities, cases, and custom objects.
  • Each record within an object represents a specific instance of that object, with its own set of fields and related data. For example, a record in the accounts object might represent a single customer account, with fields such as the account name, address, phone number, and industry type.
  • Records in Salesforce can be created, viewed, edited, and deleted by users with the appropriate permissions. They can also be searched, filtered, and sorted using various tools within the Salesforce interface.
  • Additionally, records in Salesforce can be related to one another, forming a hierarchy or network of interconnected data. For example, an opportunity record might be related to a specific account record and a specific contact record, allowing users to easily view and manage all related data within the Salesforce system.
What is a workflow in Salesforce?
  • A workflow in Salesforce is a set of rules that automates standard internal procedures and processes to save time and streamline workflows. It enables the automation of repetitive manual tasks and reduces the workload of users, allowing them to focus on more important tasks.
  • Workflows in Salesforce are typically used to automate business processes, such as sending email notifications, creating tasks, updating fields, or assigning new records to specific users. They can be triggered by specific events, such as when a record is created or updated, or based on a set schedule.
  • Workflows are created using a simple drag-and-drop interface in Salesforce, making it easy for users to define the criteria and actions for each step in the workflow. For example, a workflow can be created to automatically send an email notification to a sales manager when a new opportunity is created, or to update the status of a case when it is assigned to a support representative.
  • Overall, workflows in Salesforce can help organizations automate their internal processes, reduce manual errors, and improve overall efficiency.
What is a trigger in Salesforce?
  • A trigger in Salesforce is a piece of Apex code that is executed before or after certain events occur in the Salesforce platform, such as the insertion, update, or deletion of records.Triggers are used to automate business processes and perform custom operations on data within Salesforce.
  •  They can be used to perform complex data validations, update related records, and enforce complex business rules.Triggers are associated with a specific object in Salesforce, and can be set to execute before or after specific events occur, such as before an object is inserted or updated, or after it has been deleted.
  •  Triggers can be written using Apex code and are stored in Salesforce as metadata.Triggers are an important feature in Salesforce as they allow developers and administrators to customize and extend the platform's functionality to meet the specific needs of their organization. 
  • However, it is important to use triggers with caution and follow best practices to avoid performance issues and data integrity problems.


What is a sandbox in Salesforce?
  • In Salesforce, a sandbox is a copy of an organization's data and metadata that is used for testing and development purposes. Sandboxes are isolated from the production environment and are used to test changes to the Salesforce configuration, custom code, or integrations before they are deployed to the live environment.
  • Sandboxes are useful for testing changes without affecting live data or disrupting business operations. They can also be used to train users or to create new applications or customizations without the risk of impacting production data.
  • Salesforce provides several types of sandboxes, each with different levels of access and functionality. These include:

  • Developer Sandbox: A free sandbox designed for individual developers or small teams to build and test customizations or integrations.

  • Developer Pro Sandbox: A paid sandbox that provides more storage and functionality than the Developer Sandbox, making it suitable for larger teams or more complex projects.

  • Full Sandbox: A complete copy of the production environment, including all data and metadata. This is useful for testing complex changes or integrations.

  • Partial Copy Sandbox: A partial copy of the production environment that includes a subset of data, as specified by the administrator. This is useful for testing changes with realistic data.

  • In summary, a Salesforce sandbox is a copy of an organization's data and metadata used for testing and development purposes, and there are different types of sandboxes available depending on the specific needs of the organization
What is a Visualforce page in Salesforce?
  • A Visualforce page in Salesforce is a custom user interface component that can be used to display and interact with data from the Salesforce database. Visualforce pages are designed using a markup language that is similar to HTML, which allows developers to create customized interfaces that can be tailored to meet the specific needs of their organization.
  • Visualforce pages can be used to display a wide range of data, including records, lists, reports, and dashboards. They can also be used to collect data from users through forms and other input elements.
  • Visualforce pages are typically created using the Salesforce Developer Console, which provides a user-friendly interface for designing and editing pages. Once a Visualforce page has been created, it can be added to a Salesforce application or embedded within other pages using the Visualforce component.
  • Visualforce pages can be accessed by users with the appropriate permissions, and can be customized to display different data or functionality based on the user's role or profile. They are an important tool for developers and administrators who want to create customized user interfaces within the Salesforce platform.

What is a sharing rule in Salesforce?
  • A sharing rule in Salesforce is a tool that allows administrators to extend access to records beyond the organization-wide defaults set by the organization.
  •  Sharing rules can be used to selectively share records with groups of users or public groups based on specific criteria, such as record ownership, location, or other field values.Sharing rules can be created for standard and custom objects in Salesforce, and they can be used to grant read-only or read/write access to records.
  •  Sharing rules can also be used to grant access to users or groups outside of the organization, such as partners or customers.
  • There are two types of sharing rules in Salesforce:
  • criteria-based sharing rules  and ownership-based sharing rules. Criteria-based sharing rules allow records to be shared based on certain criteria, such as field values or record ownership. 
  • Ownership-based sharing rule s allow records to be shared with users based on the record owner or the owner's role in the organization.
  • Sharing rules can be a powerful tool for ensuring that users have the right level of access to records, regardless of their role in the organization or their location. However, it's important to use sharing rules carefully and thoughtfully, as they can also increase the complexity of the security model and make it more difficult to manage permissions over time.


What is a role hierarchy in Salesforce?
  • A role hierarchy in Salesforce is a way of defining a hierarchical relationship between different roles in an organization. This hierarchy is used to control access to data and records, as well as to determine how certain actions can be performed.
  • The role hierarchy is based on a pyramid structure, with the most senior roles at the top and the least senior roles at the bottom. Users with higher roles in the hierarchy have access to all the records that are owned by users below them in the hierarchy. They can also perform certain actions on those records, such as editing or deleting them.
  • Role hierarchies are typically used in organizations where there is a need to restrict access to certain records or data based on the user's position in the organization. For example, a sales manager might have access to all the sales data for their team, while a sales representative might only have access to their own sales data.
  • Role hierarchies can be customized to meet the specific needs of an organization. This includes defining the roles, creating rules for how data and records can be accessed, and setting up sharing rules to allow users to share data outside of their role hierarchy.

What is the difference between a contact and an account in Salesforce?

In Salesforce, a contact and an account are two separate objects that are related to each other. An account represents a company or organization, while a contact represents an individual who is associated with that account.

  • Here are some key differences between the two:

  • Ownership: An account is typically owned by a sales representative or account manager, while a contact is typically owned by the individual who created it or the person assigned to manage that contact.

  • Relationship: Accounts and contacts have a parent-child relationship in Salesforce. An account can have many related contacts, but a contact can only be associated with one account.

  • Information: An account contains information about the company or organization, such as the company name, address, and phone number. A contact contains information about the individual associated with the account, such as their name, email address, and job title.

  • Access: Accounts and contacts have different levels of access within Salesforce. A sales representative may have access to all the accounts in their territory, but may only have access to certain contacts within those accounts.

  • Marketing: Accounts are typically used for marketing purposes, such as targeting a specific company or industry. Contacts are used for more personalized communication, such as sending an email or making a phone call to an individual contact.

In summary, while accounts and contacts are related, they serve different purposes within Salesforce. Accounts represent companies or organizations, while contacts represent individuals associated with those accounts.

What is the difference between a standard object and a custom object in Salesforce?
  • A standard object in Salesforce is a pre-built object that is included in the Salesforce platform and comes with predefined fields, relationships, and functionality. Examples of standard objects include Accounts, Contacts, Opportunities, Cases, and Leads.
  • On the other hand, a custom object in Salesforce is an object that is created by a user to store data that is specific to their business needs. Custom objects can be created by adding fields, relationships, and custom functionality to the object.

The key differences between standard and custom objects in Salesforce are:

  • Pre-built vs. User-defined: Standard objects are pre-built by Salesforce and come with predefined fields and functionality, while custom objects are created by users and can be customized to fit their specific business requirements.

  • Common vs. Unique: Standard objects are common to all Salesforce organizations and are used for standard business processes, while custom objects are unique to each organization and are tailored to specific business needs.

  • Access and Permissions: Standard objects have default access and permissions settings that are determined by Salesforce, while custom objects can be customized to provide specific levels of access and permissions to users.

  • Integration: Standard objects can be easily integrated with other Salesforce features and applications, while custom objects require additional customization to integrate with other Salesforce features and applications.

In summary, standard objects are pre-built objects provided by Salesforce that support common business processes, while custom objects are user-defined objects that can be customized to fit specific business needs.

What is a custom label in Salesforce?
  • A custom label in Salesforce is a way to create a reusable text value that can be used in various parts of an organization's Salesforce instance. Custom labels can be used to replace hard-coded text values, such as field labels, validation rule error messages, or email templates, making it easier to manage and update the text values in one place.
  • Custom labels are particularly useful in multi-language organizations, as they allow administrators to create translations of the same text value for different languages. This helps ensure that users see the correct text values in their preferred language, based on their user settings.
  • Custom labels can be created by navigating to the Setup menu in Salesforce and selecting the Custom Labels option. From there, administrators can create a new custom label, define its text value, and assign translations for different languages.
  • Once a custom label is created, it can be used in various parts of Salesforce by referencing its unique name, which is automatically generated by Salesforce when the label is created. Custom labels can also be referenced in Apex code or Visualforce pages, making it easier to maintain consistent messaging and branding across an organization's Salesforce instance.
What is a picklist in Salesforce?
  • In Salesforce, a picklist is a type of field that allows users to select a value from a pre-defined list of options. Picklists are commonly used to capture data such as product categories, lead sources, or contact types, and can be used to ensure data consistency and accuracy.
  • When creating a picklist field, administrators define a list of values that users can choose from. These values can be arranged in a specific order, and can also be marked as "active" or "inactive" to control whether they appear in the picklist or not. Picklists can also be set to allow users to select multiple values, or to restrict them to choosing only one value.
  • In addition to standard picklists, Salesforce also offers dependent picklists, which allow the available options in one picklist to change based on the value selected in another picklist. This can be useful for capturing related data, such as product models and colors, or lead sources and campaigns.
  • Overall, picklists are a powerful tool for managing data in Salesforce, and can help to ensure consistency and accuracy in data entry, while also making it easier for users to find and select the appropriate values.
